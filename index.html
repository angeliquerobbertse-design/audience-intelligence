Section 01
Executive Summary

Young, urban and working. 40% of Mochachos' audience falls in the 25–34 age bracket, with 53% in full-time employment — a convenience-driven, lunch-ready segment.

Gauteng dominates at 63%. The audience is heavily concentrated in Gauteng, with secondary pockets in North West (12%) and KZN (11%). Urban fast-casual messaging is highly relevant.

TikTok leads ad receptiveness at 28%. Facebook and Instagram follow. Short-form video is the primary influence channel for this audience — and where budget should be concentrated.

34.4% want loyalty rewards. A significant audience segment is actively seeking incentive programmes — a direct product opportunity for Mochachos to capture frequency.

Weekly takeaway habit is strong. 47% order takeaways at least once a week or more. Competing directly with KFC (69%) and McDonald's (67%) on convenience and value is the core battleground.

Private vehicles and e-hailing coexist. 63% use private transport, 39% use e-hailing — suggesting drive-thru and delivery are both high-priority touchpoints.

Section 09
Strategic Campaign Opportunities
01

Lunch Convenience — The Working Professional

40% of your audience is 25–34 and 53% is fully employed. They are time-poor, convenience-driven, and making daily meal decisions. A midday digital push across IOL — targeting working hours, Gauteng-centric — directly intercepts this decision moment.

IOL Contextual Targeting
02

TikTok-First Creative with IOL Editorial Amplification

TikTok appeals to 28% of the audience for advertising — the highest of any channel. A campaign that leads with TikTok video creative, amplified by IOL entertainment and lifestyle articles, bridges social discovery with trusted publisher credibility.

Cross-Platform Strategy
03

Loyalty Programme Launch Campaign

34.4% want loyalty rewards and only 30% currently use any programme. This is a high-intent, low-satisfaction segment. A Mochachos rewards card launch — amplified via IOL lifestyle and food editorial — has measurable conversion potential.

Retention & Frequency
04

E-hailing Partnership Targeting

39% of the audience uses e-hailing services. A co-branded campaign with Bolt Food or Uber Eats — amplified through IOL tech and lifestyle coverage — directly targets the delivery-first meal decision, capturing share from KFC and McDonald's delivery arms.

Delivery Channel
05

Value Meal Messaging for Budget-Conscious Diners

Mobile spend patterns show 36.5% spend under R200/month on mobile — a cost-sensitive segment. Messaging that champions value, portion size, and price certainty across IOL news and personal finance sections captures a reader in budget-decision mode.
